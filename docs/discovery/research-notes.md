# Purpose

This document captures research findings that help us understand how member-based organizations currently manage recurring funds and how UnityFund can improve those workflows.

Research should be factual, traceable where possible, and directly relevant to product decisions.

---

# Research Areas

The current research focuses on:

* Registered Cooperative Societies
* Staff Welfare Associations
* Professional Associations
* Alumni Associations
* Recurring fund management
* Payment collection
* Financial reporting
* Member management

---

# Findings

## Finding 1 — Organizations Manage Multiple Funds

### Observation

Many member-based organizations operate more than one recurring fund at the same time.

Examples include:

* Annual Dues
* Welfare Fund
* Emergency Fund
* Savings Fund
* Building Fund
* Rotational Savings

### Impact on UnityFund

One organization must be able to create and manage multiple independent funds.

Each fund must maintain its own:

* Members
* Collection schedule
* Rules
* Reports
* Payment history

---

## Finding 2 — Financial Administration Is Highly Manual

### Observation

Many organizations still rely on combinations of:

* Excel spreadsheets
* WhatsApp groups
* Bank alerts
* Payment screenshots
* Manual reminders

Treasurers spend significant time confirming payments and updating records.

### Impact on UnityFund

Automation should focus on reducing repetitive administrative work rather than simply collecting payments.

---

## Finding 3 — Different Funds Follow Different Rules

### Observation

Organizations do not manage every fund in the same way.

Examples:

Annual dues renew membership.

Savings increase individual balances.

Emergency funds require approval before disbursement.

Rotational savings pay one member per cycle.

### Impact on UnityFund

Every fund type requires configurable business rules.

The platform should support different workflows without changing the core system.

---

## Finding 4 — Transparency Builds Trust

### Observation

Members want to know:

* Have I paid?
* How much have I contributed?
* What is the current status of the fund?
* When is the next contribution due?

Administrators need accurate reporting and visibility.

### Impact on UnityFund

Member dashboards and administrator reports should be core features rather than optional additions.

---

## Finding 5 — Payment Collection Is Only One Step

### Observation

Collecting money is not the end of the process.

Organizations also need to:

* Confirm payments
* Update member records
* Generate reports
* Approve withdrawals
* Execute payouts where applicable

### Impact on UnityFund

UnityFund should automate the complete lifecycle of a fund rather than focusing only on payment collection.

---

# Research Insights

The research reinforces the idea that organizations require software built around financial operations rather than isolated payment functionality.

The greatest opportunity is not moving money.

The greatest opportunity is simplifying the administration of recurring organizational funds.

# Questions Requiring Further Research

The following areas require additional validation:

* Typical pricing expectations for organizations.
* Average number of members per organization.
* Common approval workflows.
* Penalties for missed contributions.
* Common financial reports required by treasurers.
* Member onboarding practices.
* Existing software adoption.

---

# Product Implications

Based on the current research, UnityFund should prioritize:

* Multiple fund support.
* Configurable fund rules.
* Automated payment reconciliation.
* Member transparency.
* Administrative reporting.
* Workflow automation.

These findings should continue to evolve as additional research and customer interviews are completed.
