# Purpose

The Discovery phase exists to validate our understanding of the problem before we design or build the product.

Rather than relying on assumptions, Discovery helps us gather evidence about how member-based organizations currently manage recurring funds, the challenges they face, and how UnityFund can solve those challenges.

Every important product decision should be supported by documented research, observations, or validated assumptions.

---

# Objectives

The Discovery phase aims to:

* Understand how target organizations currently operate.
* Identify operational pain points.
* Validate product assumptions.
* Research existing solutions and competitors.
* Document industry workflows.
* Reduce product and technical risk before development.

---

# Deliverables

This folder contains:

* **assumptions.md** — Current assumptions that require validation.
* **research-notes.md** — Findings from interviews, articles, observations, and field research.
* **competitor-analysis.md** — Analysis of existing products and alternatives.

Every new idea follows this lifecycle:

Idea

↓

Assumption

↓

Research

↓

Evidence

↓

↓

Product Design

↓

Implementation

---

# Discovery Principles

* We document assumptions before treating them as facts.
* We validate important assumptions whenever possible.
* Product decisions should be based on evidence.
* Business understanding comes before technical implementation.
* Discovery continues throughout the life of the product.

---

# Success Criteria

The Discovery phase is considered successful when:

* Major assumptions are documented.
* Critical assumptions have been validated or rejected.
* Customer problems are clearly understood.
* Existing solutions have been evaluated.
* The team has enough confidence to begin detailed product design.
