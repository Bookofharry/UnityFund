# Problem Statement

## Introduction

Across Nigeria and many parts of Africa, thousands of member-based organizations manage recurring funds every week, month, and year.

These organizations include:

* Registered Cooperative Societies
* Staff Welfare Associations
* Professional Associations
* Alumni Associations
* Community Associations
* Religious Organizations

Although they serve different purposes, they all share one challenge:

They manage financial operations using disconnected and manual processes.

---

# The Current Reality

Most organizations rely on a combination of:

* Excel spreadsheets
* WhatsApp groups
* Bank alerts
* Payment screenshots
* Manual reconciliation
* Paper records

Administrators spend hours every collection cycle reminding members to pay, confirming payments, updating spreadsheets, reconciling transactions, and preparing reports.

As organizations grow, these manual processes become increasingly difficult to manage.

---

# Operational Problems

Organizations face several day-to-day operational challenges.

### Manual Collections

Administrators manually remind members to make payments every collection cycle.

### Manual Reconciliation

Payments are confirmed by checking bank alerts or payment screenshots individually.

### Poor Payment Visibility

There is no single source of truth showing who has paid, who is outstanding, and the overall status of each fund.

### Administrative Burden

Treasurers and administrators spend significant time performing repetitive administrative tasks instead of focusing on serving their members.

---

# Management Problems

Beyond daily operations, organizations struggle with financial management.

### Limited Transparency

Members often have limited visibility into the status of organizational funds.

### Inconsistent Reporting

Financial reports are frequently generated manually and may contain errors.

### Different Funds Require Different Rules

Organizations rarely operate a single fund.

Instead, they manage multiple funds simultaneously.

Examples include:

* Annual Dues
* Savings Funds
* Welfare Funds
* Emergency Funds
* Building Funds
* Investment Funds
* Rotational Savings

Each fund follows different business rules, yet most organizations attempt to manage all of them using the same spreadsheets and messaging platforms.

---

# Strategic Problems

As organizations grow, manual processes limit their ability to scale.

Growth introduces:

* More members
* More funds
* More contribution cycles
* More financial records
* More approvals
* More reporting requirements

Without software designed around these workflows, operational complexity increases significantly.

---

# Why Existing Tools Fall Short

Existing tools solve only parts of the problem.

Banking applications move money.

Payment gateways process transactions.

Spreadsheets store data.

Messaging applications send reminders.

None of these tools understand how organizational funds actually operate.

They do not understand:

* Fund-specific business rules
* Contribution cycles
* Member eligibility
* Approval workflows
* Fund lifecycles
* Organizational financial processes

As a result, organizations are forced to combine multiple disconnected tools to complete a single workflow.

---

# Why This Problem Matters

Manual financial administration creates:

* Human error
* Poor transparency
* Delayed reporting
* Increased administrative workload
* Reduced operational efficiency
* Lower member confidence

For organizations handling hundreds or thousands of members, these problems become increasingly costly.

---

# The Opportunity

Member-based organizations need more than payment processing.

They need software designed around the way they manage funds.

Rather than digitizing individual transactions, there is an opportunity to digitize the complete lifecycle of organizational funds—from member onboarding and recurring collections to reconciliation, reporting, approvals, and payouts.

---

# Our Response

UnityFund addresses this challenge by providing a centralized financial operating platform built specifically for member-based organizations.

Instead of managing recurring funds through disconnected tools, organizations can create structured fund types, define fund-specific rules, automate collections through Nomba, reconcile payments automatically, execute approved workflows, and monitor every fund from a single dashboard.

---

# Conclusion

The problem is not that organizations cannot collect money.

The problem is that they lack software built around the financial workflows required to manage recurring organizational funds.

UnityFund exists to replace fragmented financial administration with a unified, automated, and transparent operating platform.
